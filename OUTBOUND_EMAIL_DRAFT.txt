What DebtSignal is:
DebtSignal is a docs-first API concept for financing and covenant events disclosed in SEC 8-K filings and attached exhibits. The goal is to turn messy public debt disclosures into source-linked, agent-friendly event data.

Why this dataset matters:
Debt raises, amendments, waivers, defaults, covenant changes, maturity extensions, and refinancings are meaningful events for credit investors, event-driven funds, diligence teams, and automated research workflows. The problem is that these disclosures are usually buried across 8-K text and legal exhibits, with inconsistent wording and uneven structure.

What the product is promising at launch:
- A public-data, source-linked event feed concept built around SEC filings
- Docs-first API positioning with a minimal endpoint surface
- Machine-friendly response shapes with provenance, confidence, and explicit nulls
- Honest positioning around current scope and future expansion

Example endpoint coverage:
- GET /v1/events
- GET /v1/events/{event_id}
- GET /v1/issuers/{issuer_id}/events
- GET /v1/event-types
- GET /v1/docs/openapi.json

Example event coverage:
- new financing
- amendment
- waiver
- default or acceleration
- covenant change
- maturity extension
- refinancing
- repayment or termination
- collateral or guarantor change

Sample response idea:
Each event record includes issuer metadata, filing metadata, an event type, normalized extracted terms where available, source excerpts, SEC links, and confidence/completeness signals so downstream systems can automate triage while preserving a path back to the original filing.

Why the positioning is credible:
We are not claiming a full legal parser, covenant compliance engine, or live payment infrastructure. The site positions DebtSignal as a high-signal monitoring and triage layer built on public SEC disclosures.

x402-ready note:
DebtSignal is positioned as x402-ready in the future, meaning the API model is compatible with adding usage-based or agent-mediated payment flows later. This is roadmap positioning only, not a claim that billing or x402 infrastructure is already live.

Important caveats:
- Coverage is limited to publicly disclosed events in SEC filings and available exhibits.
- Issuer disclosure quality and timing vary.
- Some extracted terms may be partial, ambiguous, or null.
- Human review is still appropriate for legal, underwriting, or high-stakes investment decisions.
